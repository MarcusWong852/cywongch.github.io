Project 1 - Smart Landry

Introduction

Smart Landry is a mobile Application which aim to assist UST student who stay on campus in doing landry. By providing functions including

  • Machine availability browsing
  • Notification upon landry completion
  • Queueing system
  • Laundry balance checking
  • Custom item checklist

The goal of this app is to enhance student's experience in using the landry facilities as well as to promote smart living ideology on our campus.

Brainstorming/Gathering User POV - why we come up with this idea?

At the beginning of our disscusion several problems on the campus were being addressed.

First is the navigation problem. As campus is huge and the structure is complicated, it is often difficult to locate a room and sometime get lose before midterm/exams. Even there is exist solution to this problem, the Path Advisor, most people agree that it isn't really a effective solution to the navigation problem, as the direction indication could often could confusing in the app.

Mindmap - Navigation problem

Another campus problem that was being addressed is the queueing problem, as we also find that queueing in canteen or library is also a painful process that we often have to face.

Mindmap - Queing problems

The last campus problem, which is the one we develop our project from, is the landry room problem. From our interview and chat with our fellow UST students, many of us who stay or have stayed on the campus found that landry is a problem. Below some of the core problems of doing landry on campus.

First, knowing when to pickup clothes is inconvenience. For now, there doesn't exist any system which connects the landry machines to the internet to let us know if our landry is finished. Therefore, student will have to setup their own timer or visit the landry room to check. However, student may sometimes forget to setup the timer and visiting the landry room is very time consuming, making pickup clothes difficult. Worse, the student may entirely forget to collection their clothes and occupy the washer unnecessarily, forcing other users to wait.

Picture from our storyboard

Second, finding available machine is inconvenience. Since the only way know if there is any available washer/dryer is to visit landry, student may just waste a lot time to come and go between the landry room only to find no machine is available.

Reflection

Despite it is the first project in HCI course and my group have worked on it for only a week, I have already gain a lot and putting different HCI theories in practice while completing this project.

My role in this project is to complete the paper prototype as well as the paper prototype video. Thus, I am heavily involved in UI/UX design process of this project. In this process, I have learned how to utilize different software tools such as Figma to construct the interface of our application and make simulation of the user flow of our software. Also, this process of designing the UI encourage me to explore different existing design on the app market, which broaden my horizons and lead to further think reasons behind the existing design.

To be more specific, one thing that I have learn in terms of design thinking is how to utilize user's experience/knowledge on UI/UX in helping them to quickly adapt our UI/UX. By adopting popular UI/UX design on the market, the users can interact with our interface intuitively as they are likely to have seen/used the similar design before. Therefore, there is actually a good reason why we should copy the design elements from the others for improving user experience.

In addition, this project gave me the experience in designing a product at group zero to something with others. Although I do share experience in developing software with others from beginning stage to deployment, I was never involved in part which is about gathering the users' need and coming up with the functions. Hence, this experience will also help me in becoming a well-rounded software developer.

All in all, this is fruitful project that helped me in not only in practicing the knowledge that I have learn in the lecture but also to explore by myself and learning something outside the syllabus.
